- **D-11 S2 prose migrates `themes/cost/cfd-dynamics.md` narrative.** The full narrative from the deleted `docs/themes/cost/cfd-dynamics.md` migrates into cfd.md's §3 Cost dynamics section: four-panel explainer ("Panel [1] — Monthly generation", "Panel [2] — Unit prices", "Panel [3] — Premium per MWh", "Panel [4] — Cumulative premium"), "What the chart reveals" insight ("The CfD shield does not work..."), Caveats block (early period excluded, existing-fleet economics, gas-supply assumption). - **D-12 Adversarial lead rewrites for RO-cadence match.** Replace the existing 3-sentence Module-in-focus lead with a single-punch opening matching RO's cadence. Example draft (planner refines): "**CfD: the price floor sold as a shield — ten years in, UK consumers have paid £29 billion for CfD electricity and £14 billion more than the gas fleet would have cost for the same MWh.**" Follow-up sentence preserves the 2022-gas-crisis evidence anchor: "The premium is paid every month, including 2022 when gas was at record highs and CfD electricity still cost 7% more than the gas alternative." Two key adversarial anchors survive (£29bn paid + £14bn premium + 2022 gas-crisis test); the single-sentence-punch cadence matches RO's "The scheme you've never heard of, twice the size of the one you have — £67bn since 2002".
- **D-13 Anchor headline = £29bn paid + £14bn premium (both visible).** Both numbers appear in the opening paragraph. Phase 5 D-12 "every component visible, no component hidden" discipline applied to CfD: adversarial readers can reproduce either the total cost (£29bn) or the policy-cost-delta (£14bn) independently. The relationship between them IS the story. Avoids the trap of picking one number and burying the other — the scheme's defenders quote £29bn as "clean electricity paid for"; the scheme's critics quote £14bn as "overspend"; this page publishes both.
- **D-14 Headline FAQ section with CfD-specific questions.** Add a §9 "Headline FAQ" after §8 data & code (mirrors RO's structure at `docs/schemes/ro.md` lines 220–237). Four questions: (1) **Is £29bn / £14bn the only figure that matters?** — No; annual levy, per-MWh premium, forward commitment are equal-weight reads. (2) **Why compare to gas rather than to renewables-without-subsidy?** — Marginal-displacement question; full methodology lives at `methodology/gas-counterfactual.md`; alternative framings documented on other themes. (3) **What about 2022? Didn't the scheme work that year?** — The 2022 gas-crisis test: CfD still cost 7% more than gas even at crisis peak; premium-per-MWh inverts briefly but cumulative bill keeps climbing. (4) **How is £29bn different from LCCC's published CfD figures?** — CfD-site figures are daily fleet-weighted strike minus gas-counterfactual aggregated to £bn; LCCC publishes levy payments directly. Convention differences documented (CY axis, gas-counterfactual formula).
