```markdown
# UK Renewables Obligation (RO)

**The scheme you've never heard of, twice the size of the one you have — £X bn in RO subsidy paid by UK consumers since 2002.**

Of the total: £A bn attributable to biomass co-firing (Drax and others); £B bn from the 2021-22 mutualisation event when suppliers defaulted on ROC obligations during the energy-price crisis; NIRO Northern Ireland obligations (£C bn) are excluded from the GB headline but available via `country='NI'` filter in the Parquet. All headline components are visible and adversarially filterable.

## What is the RO?

The Renewables Obligation ran 2002-04-01 through 2017-03-31 (closed to new accreditations) and commits UK consumers to pay approximately £6 bn per year for another decade to 2037-03-31. At its peak it was twice the size of the Contracts for Difference (CfD) scheme.

The mechanism: energy suppliers are legally obliged to present a quota of Renewables Obligation Certificates (ROCs) each "scheme year" (April-March). Certified renewable generators receive ROCs per MWh of eligible generation (the "banding factor" varies by technology — onshore wind = 0.9, offshore wind = 2.0, co-fired biomass = 0.5 at peak, etc.). Suppliers either acquire ROCs from generators or pay the annual buy-out price per shortfall ROC into a central fund, which is then recycled back to ROC-holding generators. Effectively, consumers pay a commodity-priced premium (buyout + recycle, averaging ~£60-100 per ROC) embedded in their electricity bills.

The scheme closed to new accreditations in March 2017 but accredited stations continue receiving ROCs for 20 years from their accreditation date. The last station accreditations end in 2037.

## Cost dynamics (Chart S2)

![RO dynamics — the 4-panel picture](../charts/html/subsidy_ro_dynamics_twitter.png)

[Interactive version](../charts/html/subsidy_ro_dynamics.html){target="_blank"}

Four panels (all GB-only per our D-12 headline scope):

1. **Annual ROC-eligible generation (TWh).** Shows the ramp from ~2 TWh in 2002 to ~50 TWh at the scheme's peak (2017-2020), flattening as new accreditations ceased.
2. **ROC price (£/ROC) — buyout + recycle.** The consumer's cost per ROC ranged from ~£30 in the early years to >£100 during mutualisation-affected periods. The e-ROC auction clearing price (generator-revenue view) is shown as a sensitivity band — ~2-10% below the consumer cost typically.
3. **Premium per MWh (£/MWh) — consumer overpayment.** RO cost minus our gas counterfactual, divided by eligible generation. Peaks at ~£80/MWh when gas was cheap (pre-2021) and inverts briefly during the 2022 gas-price crisis.
4. **Cumulative RO bill (£bn).** The running total. A visible step up in 2021-22 from the mutualisation event: £44.0M redistributed to ROC-holders after £119.7M of supplier defaults.

## By technology (Chart S3)

![RO cost by technology](../charts/html/subsidy_ro_by_technology_twitter.png)

[Interactive version](../charts/html/subsidy_ro_by_technology.html){target="_blank"}

Top contributors over the scheme's lifetime (GB-only):
- **Offshore wind** — largest single technology by cumulative cost; 2.0 ROCs/MWh during most build-out phase.
- **Onshore wind** — lower per-MWh cost (0.9 ROCs/MWh) but high volume.
- **Biomass (dedicated / conversion)** — concentrated at Drax (coal-to-biomass conversion post-2013) and Lynemouth.
- **Biomass (co-firing)** — pre-2013 period dominated by coal-plant co-firing ROCs; philosophically contestable whether this counts as "renewable" (attribution debate noted in methodology).
- **Solar PV** — significant post-2013 ground-mounted deployment; lower per-MWh cost than wind.

## Concentration (Chart S4)

![RO cost concentration (Lorenz, GB)](../charts/html/subsidy_ro_concentration_twitter.png)

[Interactive version](../charts/html/subsidy_ro_concentration.html){target="_blank"}

RO payments are more concentrated than CfD because the scheme has been running for 20+ years. The top 3 stations typically account for ~25-30% of cumulative cost — Drax (coal-to-biomass), Lynemouth (dedicated biomass), and the largest offshore wind farms like Hornsea Project One.

## Forward commitment (Chart S5)

![RO forward-committed obligations to 2037](../charts/html/subsidy_ro_forward_projection_twitter.png)

[Interactive version](../charts/html/subsidy_ro_forward_projection.html){target="_blank"}

Every accredited RO station receives ROCs for 20 years from accreditation. Because accreditations closed in March 2017, the final year of RO eligibility is 2037-03-31. The chart shows the remaining committed cost year-by-year: ~£6 bn/yr through 2030 (most stations still within accreditation window), stepping down after 2035 as the largest accreditations wind down, with a hard cliff in 2037.

## Methodology

- **Primary cost:** `rocs_issued × (buyout_gbp_per_roc + recycle_gbp_per_roc)` for the obligation year containing each output period end (per D-08). Mutualisation additionally applies to 2021-22 only (£44.0M; per D-11).
- **Sensitivity (e-ROC):** same ROCs-issued multiplied by the e-ROC quarterly auction clearing price — a generator-revenue proxy. Difference between the two price series quantifies "convention choice" weight (~2-10%).
- **ROC source:** We use Ofgem's published `ROCs_Issued` column as the authoritative figure (per D-01). A YAML bandings table cross-checks `generation_mwh × banding_factor` and flags stations with >1% Ofgem-vs-YAML divergence for audit (`schemes/ro/validate()` D-04 Check 1).
- **Scope:** GB-only headline. NIRO (Northern Ireland) data is in the Parquet with `country='NI'`; filter to recover UK-total.
- **Gas counterfactual:** Shared with CfD. See [`methodology/gas-counterfactual.md`](../methodology/gas-counterfactual.md) for the single source of truth on the displaced-gas assumption.
- **Calendar year axis:** All charts use calendar year (D-07). Obligation year (Apr-Mar) is preserved in `station_month.parquet::obligation_year` for audit.
- **Benchmark:** Reconciled within ±3% of [REF Constable 2025 Table 1](https://www.ref.org.uk/attachments/article/390/renewables.subsidies.01.05.25.pdf) per-year figures (per D-13, D-14). Turver's Net Zero Watch paper is cited as a peer cross-check but not the test anchor.
